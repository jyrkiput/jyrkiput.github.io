Three ways of Devops

First way: The Principles of Flow

Second way: The Principles of Feedback

Thirdh way: The Principles of Continual Experimentation and Learning

The Principles of Flow

http://itrevolution.com/the-three-ways-principles-underpinning-devops/
In first way, the emphasis is on the performance of whole system. The focus is in business value streams, that are enabled by IT. In simplest form, the stream starts when a requirement is identified, then passed to development and finally deployed into production. "From left to right" The outcomes of putting the First Way into never passing a known defect to downstream work centers, never allowing local optimization to create global degradation, always seeking to increase flow, and always seeking to achieve profound understanding of the system (as per Deming).

The Principles of Feedback

http://itrevolution.com/the-three-ways-principles-underpinning-devops/
In second way, feedback loops are created from downstream to upstream. In other words, from customers, be they internal or external (operations or end users) back to start of flow. The outcomes of the Second Way include understanding and responding to all customers, internal and external, shortening and amplifying all feedback loops, and embedding knowledge where we need it. "From right to left"

The Principles of Continual Experimentation and Learning

http://itrevolution.com/the-three-ways-principles-underpinning-devops/
The final way emphasizes culture. This culture fosters two things 1. Continual experimentation, taking risks and learning from failure 2. Understanding that repetition and practice is the prerequisite to mastery. The outcomes of the Third Way include allocating time for the improvement of daily work, creating rituals that reward the team for taking risks, and introducing faults into the system to increase resilience.
